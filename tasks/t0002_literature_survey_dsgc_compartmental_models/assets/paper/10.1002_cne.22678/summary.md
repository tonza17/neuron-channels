# Two distinct types of ON directionally selective ganglion cells in the rabbit retina

## Metadata

* **File**: `files/hoshi_2011_on-dsgc.md`
* **Published**: 2011
* **Authors**: Hideo Hoshi (US), Lian-Ming Tian (US), Stephen C. Massey (US), Stephen L. Mills (US)
* **Venue**: Journal of Comparative Neurology, Vol. 519, No. 13, pp. 2509-2521
* **DOI**: `10.1002/cne.22678`

## Abstract

Mammalian retinas contain about 20 types of ganglion cells that respond to different aspects of the
visual scene, including the direction of motion of objects in the visual field. The rabbit retina
has long been thought to contain two distinct types of directionally selective (DS) ganglion cell: a
bistratified ON-OFF DS ganglion cell that responds to onset and termination of light, and an ON DS
ganglion cell, which stratifies only in the ON layer and responds only to light onset. This division
is challenged by targeted recordings from rabbit retina, which indicate that ON DS ganglion cells
occur in two discriminably different types. One of these is strongly tracer-coupled to amacrine
cells; the other is never tracer-coupled. These two types also differ in branching pattern,
stratification depth, relative latency, and transience of spiking. The sustained, uncoupled ON DS
cell ramifies completely within the lower cholinergic band and responds to nicotine with continuous
firing. In contrast, the transient, coupled ON DS ganglion cell stratifies above the cholinergic
band and is not positioned to receive major input from cholinergic amacrine cells, consistent with
its modest response to the cholinergic agonist nicotine. Much data have accrued that directional
responses in the mammalian retina originate via gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) release from the
dendrites of starburst amacrine cells. If there is an ON DS ganglion cell that does not stratify in
the starburst band, this suggests that its GABA-dependent directional signals may be generated by a
mechanism independent of starburst amacrine cells.

## Overview

This paper challenges the long-standing view that the rabbit retina contains a single ON DS ganglion
cell type by demonstrating that ON DS cells comprise two morphologically and physiologically
distinct subtypes. Using targeted acridine-orange staining of living retinas combined with loose
patch electrophysiology and intracellular Neurobiotin/Lucifer Yellow injection, the authors recorded
from and characterised 45-52 cells of each type -- the largest systematic comparison of ON DS
subtypes at the time of publication.

The two subtypes differ on six independent criteria: tracer-coupling pattern, dendritic
stratification depth within the inner plexiform layer (IPL), branching morphology (dendritic
density, retroflexive processes, dendritic crossings), response latency, temporal transience, and
pharmacological sensitivity to nicotine. Coupling is binary (always 0 vs. 60-190 coupled amacrine
cells) and stratification depth, differing by only ~3 um, correctly predicts relative position in
100% of direct dendritic crossing comparisons.

The uncoupled, sustained subtype ramifies within the ON cholinergic band and cofasciculates with
starburst amacrine dendrites, placing it in the canonical starburst-GABA direction-selectivity
pathway. The coupled, transient subtype stratifies distal to the ChAT band and does not
cofasciculate with starburst processes, implying its directional selectivity arises by a distinct
non-starburst mechanism. Despite these circuit differences both subtypes share the same three
cardinal preferred axes, the same preferred bar velocity (~100 um/s), and statistically
indistinguishable DSI (~0.66-0.67), showing that output tuning is conserved even when the upstream
mechanism differs.

## Architecture, Models and Methods

### Animals and Retinal Preparation

Adult albino rabbits, anesthetised with urethane (1.5 g/kg i.p.) then humanely killed. Eyes removed,
retinas placed ganglion-cell-side up on black membrane filters in a perfusion chamber superfused
with Ames medium bubbled 95% O2/5% CO2. Cells identified with acridine orange nuclear staining; ON
DS cells targeted by soma morphology (medium-large ovular somata, nuclei filling ~50% of soma,
sometimes with crescent appearance).

### Electrophysiology

Loose patch recordings (4-6 MOhm borosilicate pipettes, NPI EPC-03X amplifier at 1000x gain, 60 Hz
notch filter). Data acquired with Experimenter 5.0. DSI = (p - n) / (p + n) where p and n are
preferred and null spike rates. Transience index = (firing rate in second half of 400 ms stimulus) /
(firing rate in first half); larger values indicate more sustained firing. Visual stimulation via
Lucivid monitor projected through 4x objective: 50 um bars at 200 um/s in 22.5 or 45 degree
increments from 0 to 315 degrees; spots 30-900 um for receptive field mapping.

### Intracellular Staining and Immunohistochemistry

Sharp electrodes with 0.5% Lucifer Yellow and 3.5% Neurobiotin (positive current 1 nA, 3 Hz, 10
min). Tissue fixed in 4% paraformaldehyde (1 h), visualised with streptavidin-Cy3 (1:1000).
Triple-labelling for ChAT (Millipore 1:100) and GABA (Immunosolution 1:1000) with Cy3/Cy5/ Alexa488
secondary antibodies. Confocal imaging: Zeiss LSM 510, 0.3 um optical sections.

### Morphological Analysis

Sholl analysis (ImageJ plugin, 10 um annuli from soma centre, counting dendritic intersections),
retroflexive terminal process counts, and dendritic self-crossing counts on 45 coupled and 51-52
uncoupled cells restricted to inferior retina to minimise eccentricity variability. Stratification
depth measured as % IPL in z-reconstructions referenced to ChAT bands (ON band at ~74% IPL depth).
Dendritic area compared across eccentricities using linear regression and ANOVA. Fourteen cells of
each type were paired (recorded then stained).

### Sample Sizes

n = 45 coupled (transient) and n = 51-52 uncoupled (sustained) ON DS GCs for morphology; n = 14 of
each type for paired physiology + morphology; n = 3 per type for nicotine pharmacology.

## Results

* **Tracer coupling (binary)**: Uncoupled type: **0 amacrine cells** in all 52 cells; coupled type:
  **60-190** coupled amacrine cells (mean = **102**, SD = 30.4, n = 45, max = 190). No overlap.
* **Coupled amacrine populations**: Two types, both GABA-positive -- axon-bearing (diameter **8.7
  +/- 0.58 um**, n = 19) and multistratified (**9.72 +/- 0.58 um**, n = 17); significantly different
  (t = 4.98, df = 34, p < 0.001).
* **Stratification depth**: Coupled type: **57% IPL** (SD = 3.7%, n = 5); uncoupled type: **77%
  IPL** (SD = 5.0%, n = 3). ~3 um physical separation for 15 um average IPL width. Depth ordering
  confirmed 100% in dendrite crossings (31/31 between types; 9/9 coupled vs. ON-OFF DS; 11/13
  uncoupled vs. ON-OFF DS).
* **Retroflexive terminal processes**: Coupled: **2.4 +/- 1.7**; uncoupled: **14.7 +/- 5.5** (t(94)
  = 14.52, p < 0.001).
* **Dendritic self-crossings**: Coupled: **4.6 +/- 3.3**; uncoupled: **16.5 +/- 6.1** (t(94) =
  11.71, p < 0.001). These two measures produce complete population separation.
* **Dendritic area**: Uncoupled type **8.6% larger** on average; eccentricity slope 0.0605
  (uncoupled) vs. 0.0503 (coupled) mm2/mm (p < 0.001). Both increase linearly with eccentricity.
* **Response latency to peak**: Uncoupled: **381.0 +/- 49.3 ms** (SE); coupled: **70.7 +/- 7.7 ms**
  (t = 25.2, df = 16, p < 0.01).
* **DSI**: Uncoupled: **0.66 +/- 0.27**; coupled: **0.67 +/- 0.30** -- not significantly different.
  Both share three cardinal preferred axes.
* **Preferred bar velocity**: Both types ~**100 um/s** (slower than ON-OFF DS cells).
* **Nicotine response**: Uncoupled cells show dramatic spike rate increase (n = 3, similar to ON-OFF
  DS cells); coupled cells show only modest increase (n = 3).

## Innovations

### Discovery of Two Morphologically Distinct ON DS Subtypes

The paper establishes definitively that the rabbit ON DS population contains two subtypes separable
on six independent criteria. The binary tracer-coupling measure alone provides perfect
classification, resolving decades of morphological variability in prior MTN-projection tracer
studies (Buhl and Peichl 1986; Pu and Amthor 1990).

### Non-Starburst Direction Selectivity Mechanism

The coupled (transient) subtype stratifies above the ChAT band and lacks starburst cofasciculation,
implying its GABAergic directional input does not come from starburst amacrine cells. This is the
first morphological evidence for a non-starburst direction-selectivity mechanism in a mammalian ON
DS ganglion cell.

### Quantitative Morphological Classification Tool

Retroflexive terminal processes and dendritic self-crossings produce complete population separation
across 96 cells in a 2D scatter plot. These measures are directly usable as morphological
constraints for computational models when trying to reproduce one of the two subtypes.

## Datasets

No public datasets were created or deposited. Original electrophysiological recordings and confocal
morphological data obtained from adult albino rabbit retinas at the University of Texas Health
Science Center at Houston. No reconstruction files or raw traces were deposited publicly.
Morphologies compared against published drawings from Buhl and Peichl (1986), Pu and Amthor (1990),
Ackert et al. (2006, 2009), and Kanjhan and Sivyer (2010).

## Main Ideas

* **Two ON DS subtypes must be distinguished for modelling**: Sustained/uncoupled (within ChAT band,
  starburst-dependent) and transient/coupled (above ChAT band, non-starburst) have distinct
  dendritic architectures. A compartmental model must commit to one subtype for morphological
  constraints to be valid.
* **Uncoupled (sustained) subtype is the canonical starburst-GABA target**: It stratifies in the
  starburst band, cofasciculates with ChAT processes, and has high dendritic density (14.7
  retroflexive processes, 16.5 self-crossings) -- the complex branching geometry relevant to
  cable-theory accounts of direction selectivity.
* **Stratification depth constrains input positioning**: ~77% IPL places uncoupled cells in the ChAT
  band, so GABA inputs in the model should be co-localised with the starburst layer. The ~20% IPL
  depth difference between subtypes would substantially shift input geometry.
* **DSI ~0.66 and ~100 um/s preferred velocity are quantitative validation targets**: Both subtypes
  achieve similar DSI and velocity preference despite different circuit origins. These constrain
  wave speed and EPSP/IPSP temporal parameters for the wave-stimulus protocol.
* **Dendritic area scales with eccentricity at slope 0.0605 mm2/mm** for the uncoupled subtype. When
  selecting a morphological reconstruction, the recording eccentricity determines expected dendritic
  field size.

## Summary

Hoshi et al. (2011) demonstrate that the rabbit ON DS ganglion cell population, treated as a single
type for decades, actually comprises two distinct subtypes separable on six independent criteria:
tracer-coupling pattern, IPL stratification depth, dendritic branching complexity, response latency,
temporal transience, and cholinergic pharmacology. The study uses targeted acridine-orange-guided
recording and intracellular Neurobiotin/Lucifer Yellow injection in isolated rabbit retinas,
characterising 45-52 cells of each subtype -- the largest systematic ON DS morphology-physiology
dataset at the time of publication.

The uncoupled (sustained) subtype ramifies within the ON cholinergic band (~77% IPL depth),
cofasciculates with starburst amacrine dendrites, fires with long latency (381 ms peak) and
sustained character, is never tracer-coupled, and responds strongly to nicotine -- consistent with
the starburst-GABA direction-selectivity model. The coupled (transient) subtype stratifies distal to
the ChAT band (~57% IPL depth), does not cofasciculate with starburst processes, fires with short
latency (71 ms) and transient character, is gap-junction coupled to 60-190 GABA-positive amacrine
cells, and shows minimal nicotine sensitivity -- implying a non-starburst directional mechanism.

Key quantitative findings: retroflexive terminal processes 14.7 vs. 2.4 (t(94) = 14.52, p < 0.001),
dendritic self-crossings 16.5 vs. 4.6 (t(94) = 11.71, p < 0.001), stratification 57% vs. 77% IPL
depth (~3 um separation, confirmed 100% in direct crossings), and response latency 70.7 vs. 381.0 ms
(p < 0.01). The two morphological measures together produce complete population separation across 96
cells. Despite these mechanistic differences, both subtypes produce equivalent directional output:
three cardinal preferred axes, ~100 um/s preferred velocity, and DSI ~0.66-0.67.

For the current project modelling a single ON DSGC compartmentally and optimising against a target
angle-to-AP-frequency tuning curve, this paper provides essential morphological constraints. The
uncoupled (sustained) subtype -- with high dendritic density, stratification within the starburst
band, and starburst-GABA input geometry -- is the appropriate target cell type. Key validation
benchmarks: DSI ~0.66, preferred velocity ~100 um/s, peak response latency ~381 ms, stratification
at ~77% IPL depth, eccentricity-area slope 0.0605 mm2/mm. These constrain the morphology, wave-
stimulus parameters, and response targets for compartmental simulation.
